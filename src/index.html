About the Mink Hills

“The Mink Hills is a term used locally to describe a large relatively undeveloped tract of land in the towns of Warner, Hopkinton, Henniker and Bradford. The entire track encompasses approximately 26,800 acres, of which approximately 14,376 acres lie in Warner, comprising 40% of the town.”

- From the Mink Hills Recreational Study (pdf)

Meet The Candidates

Representative
Tony Caplan

anthonycaplan1@gmail.com

facebook.com/Minkhillsrep

Born overseas, (Caracas, Venezuela), to American parents. High school and college in Connecticut. Worked as a journalist in Latin America and Europe. Moved to Henniker, NH in 2000. Raised three children on a smallholding with vegetables, apples and sheep. Chair of the Henniker Energy Committee and Henniker Democratic Town Committee. Legislative interests include climate protection and the environment, worker's rights, sustainable housing and transportation issues.

Eileen Kelly

Eileen4NH@gmail.com

Eileen4NH

Eileen served as Chief of Staff to the New Hampshire Speaker of the House from 2018 - 2020. For more than nineteen years, Eileen was the Senior Legislative Policy Assistant for the House Democratic Leadership and Caucus at the NH House of Representatives. 

Eileen currently serves on the Board of Directors for NH Hunger Solutions and is the Chair of the Bradford Democrats.  Eileen also served on the Board of Directors for the New Hampshire Coalition Against Domestic and Sexual Violence including as Board Chair; the Bradford Town Hall Restoration Committee, former Chair; the Town of Bradford Needs Assessment Committee and is currently an election official for Bradford.  Eileen holds a BA in English from New England College, summa cum laude. She has two adult sons and her Mom recently celebrated her 96th birthday! 

Representative
Stephanie Payeur

stephanie.payeur@gmail.com

facebook.com/hennikerdemocrats.org/

I am thrilled to be running for re-election to the NH State House of Representatives for Merrimack County District 8.   I enjoyed my first two-year term and I hope you will send me back in 2024!  One of the good things with voting for an incumbent is that voters can clearly see my voting record on all issues. 

I have lived in Henniker for 21 years.  I was born and raised in Manchester NH.  I graduated from UNH twice- I received my Bachelor of Science in Medical Laboratory Science, and later went back for my MBA. I work in healthcare management and have a solid background in science. I am a member of the Henniker Rotary Club and also a volunteer with the Henniker Food Pantry. I have two sons that graduated from JSRHS and also UNH with degrees in Mechanical Engineering. Both of my sons are Officers in the US Space Force and I am incredibly proud of them!
